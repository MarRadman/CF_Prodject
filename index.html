CrossFit For Beginners: 9 Essential CrossFit Exercises To Get Started

Is CrossFit Good for Beginners?

In general, most CrossFit workouts are high-intensity, strenuous workouts that combine cardio, strengthening, metabolic, speed, power and plyometric exercises all in one. For this reason, CrossFit for beginners can be a bit too intense if you are jumping into an advanced CrossFit class or not deliberately selecting beginner CrossFit exercises.

With that said, certain CrossFit movements can absolutely be incorporated into a beginnerâ€™s fitness routine. As with starting any type of fitness program, CrossFit for beginners is best approached progressively and gradually, beginning with CrossFit movements that are easy to master from a technical standpoint and not extremely physically demanding. As you build up your strength, stamina, endurance and power, you can progress your way through more challenging CrossFit exercises.

One of the benefits of trying CrossFit for beginners is that a lot of the CrossFit exercises are designed around functional movements and foundational fitness movement patterns that will help you in everyday life as well as build more advanced workout progressions.

Generally, the list of CrossFit exercises can be divided into three different categories: bodyweight exercises, barbell exercises and Olympic lifting exercises. Although some of the movements in all three categories can potentially be doable in CrossFit workouts for beginners, because the Olympic lifts tend to be the most technical and the most unfamiliar to novice CrossFit athletes, it is often best to start with the bodyweight exercises.

Bodyweight exercises will get you familiar with how to move your body and activate the intended muscles before adding additional weight. Plus, many of the popular bodyweight exercises in Crossfit for beginners combined cardio and strengthening for a well-rounded beginner CrossFit workout.
